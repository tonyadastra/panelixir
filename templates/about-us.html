This website is made by Tony and Lola, aiming to spread love and hope to everyone during the coronavirus pandemic.

Tony Liu

Tony is a 16-year-old teenager who loves computer science. He was an exchange student to Dwight School New York when the city became the global epicenter of COVID-19. During that time, he visited webpages tracking the number of cases in each country over five times a day. Seeing the number of cases growing swiftly, he thought he might be able to do something for the community. In a conversation with his daddy, he was inspired by the idea of tracking the progress of the vaccines instead of the number of cases. However, due to limited time and resources, the first attempt of creating the website wasn't successful. During summer vacation, he took the S-14A course at Harvard Summer School that taught him a lot on building interactive web applications. Together with his aunt Lola, an undergraduate at UC San Diego, they spent nearly a month on building this website, aiming to spread positivity to people during the pandemic.
